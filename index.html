Fokir's Journey
Following an Irrawaddy dolphin through the Mekong Basin
1
Kampi Pool, Kratie — Base Station
A pod of Irrawaddy dolphins surfaces at their home pool. Click any dolphin to view monitoring station data — water quality, sediment, and fisheries readings for this stretch of river.
2
Mekong Transit — The Survey
Fokir swims downstream on a survey run. When the dolphin dives, echolocation activates — the view shifts to sonar and hidden underwater hazards (gillnets, carcasses, noise pollution) are revealed by the pulse.
3
Phnom Penh Confluence — Hazard Report
At the confluence, Fokir uploads survey data to a monitoring station. Fishing boats receive the hazard report. Watch the dolphin glow golden with karma as the data transfer completes.
4
Tonle Sap — Homecoming
The pod gathers at Tonle Sap lake beside a Cambodian stilt house. The golden dolphin carries accumulated karma. The right panel shows the family whose livelihood depends on co-fishing with the dolphins.

Scenarios & Indicators
Cooperative Recovery — Models outcomes if transboundary conservation efforts succeed: reduced pollution, fishing regulation enforcement, dam flow agreements. Water quality improves, fish populations rebound.
Accelerated Decline — Models outcomes under continued hydropower expansion, unregulated fishing, and increasing agricultural runoff. Dissolved oxygen drops, sediment loads spike, species disappear.
